# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mindline is for privacy-first teams that need direct, trusted communication without server-side message storage. Users are small groups who care about secure peer-to-peer rooms, but also need the interface to make unusual behavior clear before it surprises them.

## Product Purpose

Mindline exists to make radical transparency usable: room participants can see live drafts as they are typed, while messages remain private from centralized services through local-first, encrypted, peer-to-peer communication. Success means users understand what is visible, who can join, whether peers are connected, and what is stored locally without slowing down the create, join, and chat flow.

## Brand Personality

Warm, honest, human. The product should feel candid about its unusual interaction model, protective of user trust, and emotionally grounded rather than cold, paranoid, or theatrical.

## Anti-references

Mindline should not look like a generic SaaS dashboard, a toy chat app, a paranoid security tool, or a corporate productivity suite. Avoid interchangeable startup polish, childish messenger tropes, fear-driven security aesthetics, and bland enterprise collaboration patterns.

## Design Principles

- Radical clarity before delight: explain live drafts, room access, local storage, and peer sync at the moment they matter.
- Privacy without paranoia: communicate security and connection state plainly, without fear imagery or hacker aesthetics.
- Human transparency: make exposed typing feel intentional, respectful, and shared, not accidental or punitive.
- Flow stays fast: preserve the current zero-account create and join path while improving confidence.
- Status is behavior: every visible status should answer what is happening now and what the user can do next.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Preserve keyboard access, visible focus states, readable contrast, and screen-reader accessible labels for controls and connection states. Respect reduced motion preferences. Do not rely on color alone to communicate delivery, connection, or warning states.
