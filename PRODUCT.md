# PRODUCT.md

## Product purpose

Mindline is the fastest way for two or a few people to talk privately. Open a
link, you're in a room, end of onboarding. No accounts, no server storing
anything: messages travel peer-to-peer, end-to-end encrypted. The twist that
gives the product its name: you see the other person's message forming as they
type it, character by character. A conversation feels like sharing a wire, not
exchanging letters.

## Users

- Pairs and small groups (2-5) who want a quick, private side-channel:
  friends, partners, founders, source-and-journalist, support call + chat.
- Privacy-conscious people who distrust hosted chat but won't install anything.
- Both peers are typically online at the same time. This is presence-centric
  chat, closer to a phone call than to email. The product should lean into
  that instead of apologizing for it.
- Devices: phones first (one hand, on the move, flaky networks), laptops second.

## Register

product

## Tone

Calm, intimate, exact. The interface should feel like a quiet, well-lit room
where the only thing that matters is the other person's words appearing.
Security is communicated through legibility and restraint, not through
padlocks, shields, or hacker aesthetics. Delight lives in the live-typing
moment, not in chrome.

## Anti-references

- Neon-on-black "encrypted chat" hacker cliché (Matrix-rain energy).
- Generic SaaS slop: gradient heroes, glassmorphism cards, padlock icons.
- Discord/Slack-style heavy chrome with sidebars and settings sprawl.
- WhatsApp-clone green bubbles. This is not a messenger replacement; it is a
  live wire between people.

## Strategic principles

1. **The draft stream is the hero.** Live typing is the reason this product
   exists. It must be staged as a first-class moment in the message flow, not
   a footnote indicator.
2. **Seconds-to-talking is the metric.** Every screen, copy line, and
   permission prompt is judged by how much it delays two people talking.
3. **Trust through honesty.** Security claims in UI and docs must match the
   code exactly. Show what is protected and what is not, in plain words.
4. **Thumb-first.** Every core action reachable one-handed on a phone;
   keyboard, safe areas, and viewport handled natively-well.
5. **Presence over history.** The room is alive when people are in it.
   History is a convenience, not the point, and never leaves the devices.
